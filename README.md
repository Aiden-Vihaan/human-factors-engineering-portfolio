## Human Factors Engineering Portfolio

About This Portfolio

Hello, and welcome to my Human Factors Engineering Portfolio.

My name is Aiden Vihaan, and I am a Bachelor of Computer Applications graduate with a growing interest in understanding how humans interact with technology. While my academic background is rooted in computer science and software development, I became increasingly curious about a question that often receives less attention than technology itself:

How do people think, decide, make mistakes, learn, and interact with the systems we design?

This question led me to explore Human Factors Engineering, Cognitive Psychology, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Usability Engineering, and Human-Centered Design.

I created this repository to document my learning journey, research notes, case studies, and analyses as I prepare for graduate studies in Human Factors Engineering. Rather than treating these topics as isolated subjects, I aim to understand how psychology, design, engineering, and technology work together to create systems that are safe, efficient, accessible, and intuitive for people.

---

Why This Portfolio Exists

As someone transitioning from a computer applications background into Human Factors Engineering, I wanted to go beyond simply completing online courses and collecting certificates.

My goal is to actively engage with the field by:

- Analyzing real-world human error incidents
- Studying the cognitive processes behind decision-making
- Exploring usability and user experience principles
- Investigating the relationship between humans and automated systems
- Understanding how technology can be designed around human capabilities and limitations

This portfolio serves as a record of that exploration.

Throughout this repository, I focus on several interconnected topics:

Studying how people interact with digital interfaces and how these interactions can be improved.

Learning Philosophy

I believe that good technology is not defined solely by technical sophistication.

A system can be powerful, innovative, and technically correct, yet still fail if it does not account for human needs, limitations, and behavior.

The most effective technologies are those that complement human abilities, support informed decision-making, and remain usable under real-world conditions.

This belief motivates my interest in Human Factors Engineering and continues to guide the work documented in this portfolio.
